# Pregnancy and Nutrition

When you're pregnant, nutrition is more important than ever. You need more of many important nutrients than you did before pregnancy. Making healthy food choices every day will help you give your baby what they need to develop and will help make sure that you and your baby gain a healthy amount of weight.

## Key Nutrients During Pregnancy

### Folic Acid
Folic acid is a B vitamin that may help prevent neural tube defects (birth defects of the brain or spine). Before pregnancy, you need 400 mcg per day. During pregnancy and when breastfeeding, you need 600 mcg per day. It is hard to get this amount from foods alone, so you will most likely need to take a supplement. Sources: leafy green vegetables, fortified cereals, beans, citrus fruits.

### Iron
Iron is important for your baby's growth and brain development. During pregnancy, the amount of blood in your body increases, so you need more iron. Symptoms of iron deficiency include fatigue, weakness, and dizziness. Sources: lean red meat, poultry, fish, beans, iron-fortified cereals.

### Calcium
Calcium during pregnancy can reduce your risk of preeclampsia and builds up your baby's bones and teeth. Pregnant adults should get 1,000 mg of calcium a day. Pregnant teenagers (ages 14-18) need 1,300 mg per day. Sources: dairy products, fortified plant milks, broccoli, kale.

### Vitamin D
Vitamin D helps calcium build your baby's bones and teeth. All women, pregnant or not, should be getting 600 IU of vitamin D per day. Sources: fortified milk, fatty fish, sunlight exposure.

### Protein
You need more protein when you are pregnant. Healthy sources include beans, peas, eggs, lean meats, seafood, and unsalted nuts and seeds.

## Prenatal Vitamins

If you're pregnant, you shouldn't take regular vitamins. They may have too much or too little of the vitamins you need. Prenatal vitamins are a special kind of vitamin formulated for pregnancy. Keep in mind that taking too much of a supplement can be harmful. For example, very high levels of vitamin A can cause birth defects. Only take vitamins and mineral supplements that your health care provider recommends.

## General Nutrition Guidelines

During pregnancy, you should: eat a variety of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and dairy; drink plenty of water; eat regular meals and snacks; limit caffeine; avoid alcohol completely; avoid raw or undercooked fish, eggs, and meat; avoid unpasteurized dairy products and juices; avoid deli meats and hot dogs unless heated until steaming; limit fish high in mercury (shark, swordfish, king mackerel, tilefish).

## Weight Gain

How much weight you should gain depends on your pre-pregnancy weight. Your provider will tell you what is right for you. General guidelines: underweight women may need to gain 28-40 pounds, normal weight women 25-35 pounds, overweight women 15-25 pounds, and obese women 11-20 pounds.

## Morning Sickness

Nausea and vomiting are very common in early pregnancy. To help manage morning sickness: eat small, frequent meals; eat bland, easy-to-digest foods; avoid foods with strong odors; drink fluids between meals rather than with meals; try ginger tea or ginger candies. If you cannot keep any food or liquids down, contact your provider.

## Clinical Language Guide
- "I can't keep anything down" → "I'm experiencing persistent nausea and vomiting (hyperemesis gravidarum if severe) and unable to maintain adequate nutrition/hydration"
- "I'm not gaining enough weight" → "I'm concerned about inadequate weight gain during pregnancy"
- "I'm gaining too much weight" → "I'd like to discuss my weight gain trajectory"
- "I feel tired and dizzy all the time" → "I'm experiencing fatigue and dizziness that may be related to iron deficiency anemia"
- "I don't know what I should be eating" → "I'd like nutritional counseling for pregnancy"
- "I can't afford healthy food" → "I need help accessing nutritious food" (ask about WIC - Women, Infants, and Children program)
- "I'm vegetarian/vegan" → "I follow a plant-based diet and want to ensure adequate prenatal nutrition"
